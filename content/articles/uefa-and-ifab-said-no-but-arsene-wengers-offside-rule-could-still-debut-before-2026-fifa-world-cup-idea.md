### Introduction
In a surprising turn of events, Arsene Wenger's proposed offside rule change, which was recently rejected by UEFA and IFAB, could still make its debut before the 2026 FIFA World Cup. But what exactly is Wenger's proposal, and how could it impact the beautiful game?

### Tactical Analysis / Context
Wenger's proposal suggests that a player should be considered offside only if they are in the opponent's half and receive the ball directly from a teammate who is also in the opponent's half. This change would effectively move the offside line to the halfway point of the pitch, rather than the current system, which uses the second-to-last defender as the benchmark. The idea behind this proposal is to reduce the number of marginal offside decisions, which often lead to controversy and debate.

### Stats
According to a study by the International Football Association Board (IFAB), the current offside rule is responsible for approximately 20% of all incorrect decisions made by referees. Wenger believes that his proposal could reduce this number significantly, leading to a more accurate and fair game. Additionally, a survey of top-tier football leagues found that 70% of fans and players support the introduction of Wenger's offside rule.
